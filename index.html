A.P.J Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

Missile Man Of India

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam was given the nickname "Missile Man of India" for his significant contributions to India's missile development programs, including the Agni and Prithvi missiles. His leadership and dedication to advancing India's technological capabilities in the field of defense and space earned him widespread respect and recognition. Apart from his achievements in the field of science and technology, Dr. Kalam was also known for his inspirational speeches and his commitment to education. The term "Missile Man of India" refers to Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam, who served as the President of India from 2002 to 2007. Dr. Kalam was an aerospace engineer and played a crucial role in India's civilian space program and military missile development efforts. He was a key figure in the development of India's ballistic missile technology and was involved in the successful test-firing of various missiles.

Biographies

  • Abdul Kalam was born into a humble Tamil Muslim family. His father was a boat owner, and his mother was a housewife.
  • After completing his education, Kalam joined the Defense Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and later the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO).
  • In 2002, A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was elected as the President of India. He was widely respected for his humility, integrity, and dedication to education and development
  • Known as the "People's President," Kalam was admired for his approachability and his efforts to connect with citizens, particularly the youth.
  • A.P.J. Abdul Kalam left a lasting legacy as a scientist, leader, and inspirational figure.
